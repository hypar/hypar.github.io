1 Numerical Method
2 ================
3 
4 HyPar solves the following partial differential equation (PDE) using a conservative finite-difference
5 algorithm on a Cartesian grid.
6 \f{equation}{
7  \frac {\partial {\bf u}} {\partial t} = {\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right) + {\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) + {\bf F}_{\rm sou}\left({\bf u}\right)
8 \f}
9 where \f${\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\f$ is the hyperbolic term, \f${\bf F}_{\rm par}\f$ is the parabolic term, and
10 \f${\bf F}_{\rm sou}\f$ is the source term. Each of these is discretized in space as described below, to
11 obtain the following semi-discrete ordinary differential equation (ODE) in time:
12 \f{equation}{
13  \frac {d {\bf u}} {d t} = \hat{\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right) + \hat{\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) + \hat{\bf F}_{\rm sou}\left({\bf u}\right)
14 \f}
15 where \f$\hat{\left(\cdot\right)}\f$ represents the spatially discretized terms. The governing PDE can be
16 of any space dimension. The semi-discrete ODE is integrated in time by
17 + Using native time integrators (see timeintegration.h, Solve()).
18 + PETSc (https://www.mcs.anl.gov/petsc/) - specifically the TS module (see petscinterface.h, SolvePETSc()).
19 
20 Hyperbolic term
21 ---------------
22 
23 The hyperbolic term is of the following form:
24 \f{equation}{
25  {\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right) = -\sum_{d=0}^{D-1} \frac{\partial {\bf f}_d\left({\bf u}\right)}{\partial x_d}
26 \f}
27 and is discretized as:
28 \f{equation}{
29  {\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right) \approx - \sum_{d=0}^{D-1} \frac{1}{\Delta x_d} \left[ \hat{\bf f}_{d,j+1/2} - \hat{\bf f}_{d,j-1/2} \right]
30 \f}
31 where \f$d\f$ is the spatial dimension, \f$D\f$ is the total number of spatial dimensions, \f$j\f$ denotes the grid index along \f$d\f$. This
32 is implemented in HyperbolicFunction().
33 
34 The numerical approximation \f$\hat{\bf f}_{d,j+1/2}\f$ of the primitive of the flux \f${\bf f}_d\left({\bf u}\right)\f$ at the grid interfaces \f$j+1/2\f$ is expressed
35 as
36 \f{equation}{
37  \hat{\bf f}_{d,j+1/2} = \mathcal{U}\left(\hat{\bf f}^L_{d,j+1/2},\hat{\bf f}^R_{d,j+1/2},\hat{\bf u}^L_{d,j+1/2},\hat{\bf u}^R_{d,j+1/2}\right)
38 \f}
39 where \f$\mathcal{U}\f$ is an upwinding function. #HyPar::Upwind points to the physical model-specific upwinding function that implements
40 \f$\mathcal{U}\f$, and is set by the initialization function of a specific physical model (for example Euler1DInitialize()). The physical model
41 is specified by setting #HyPar::model (read from \a "solver.inp" in ReadInputs()).
42 
43 \f$\hat{\bf f}^{L,R}_{d,j+1/2}\f$ are the left- and right-biased numerically interpolated values of the primitive of the flux \f${\bf f}_d\left({\bf u}\right)\f$
44 at the grid interfaces and are computed using #HyPar::InterpolateInterfacesHyp. They are initialized in InitializeSolvers() based on the value of
45 #HyPar::spatial_scheme_hyp (read from \a "solver.inp" in ReadInputs()).
46 
47 #HyPar::HyperbolicFunction points to HyperbolicFunction().

49 Parabolic term
50 --------------
51 
52 The parabolic term can take two different forms as described below:-
53 
54 \b No \b cross-derivatives: In this form, the parabolic term is of the following form:
55 \f{equation}{
56  {\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) = \sum_{d=0}^{D-1} \frac {\partial^2 {\bf g}_d\left({\bf u}\right)} {\partial x_d^2}
57 \f}
58 where \f$d\f$ is the spatial dimension, and \f$D\f$ is the total number of spatial dimensions. If the parabolic function is
59 in this form, then the physical model must specify #HyPar::GFunction which must point to the function that computes
60 \f${\bf g}_d\left({\bf u}\right)\f$. In this case, the spatial discretization is carried out in one of two ways:-
61 
62 + Conservative discretization, implemented in ParabolicFunctionCons1Stage() and invoked by setting #HyPar::spatial_type_par
63  to #_CONS_1STAGE_: The parabolic term is discretized as
64 \f{equation}{
65  {\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) \approx \sum_{d=0}^{D-1} \frac {1}{\Delta x_d^2} \left[ \hat{\bf g}_{d,j+1/2} - \hat{\bf g}_{d,j-1/2} \right]
66 \f}
67  where \f$j\f$ denotes the grid index along \f$d\f$. \f$\hat{\bf g}_{d,j+1/2}\f$ is the numerical approximation to the second primitive
68  of \f${\bf g}_d\left({\bf u}\right)\f$ and is computed using #HyPar::InterpolateInterfacesPar.
69 
70 + Non-conservative, 1-Stage discretization, implemented in ParabolicFunctionNC1Stage() and invoked by setting #HyPar::spatial_type_par
71  to #_NC_1STAGE_: The parabolic term is discretized as
72 \f{equation}{
73  {\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) \approx \sum_{d=0}^{D-1} \frac {1}{\Delta x_d^2} \left[ \mathcal{L}_d\left({\bf g}_d\right) \right]
74 \f}
75  where \f$\mathcal{L}\f$ represents the finite-difference Laplacian operator, and is computed using #HyPar::SecondDerivativePar.
76 
77 \b With \b cross-derivatives: In this form, the parabolic term is of the following form:
78 \f{equation}{
79  {\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) = \sum_{d1=0}^{D-1}\sum_{d2=0}^{D-1} \frac {\partial^2 h_{d1,d2}\left({\bf u}\right)} {\partial x_{d1} \partial x_{d2}}
80 \f}
81 where \f$d1,d2\f$ are spatial dimensions, \f$D\f$ is the total number of spatial dimensions. If the parabolic function is
82 in this form, then the physical model must specify #HyPar::HFunction which must point to the function that computes
83 \f${\bf h}_{d1,d2}\left({\bf u}\right)\f$. In this case, the spatial discretization is carried out in one of two ways:-
84 
85 + Non-conservative 2-stage discretization, implemented in ParabolicFunctionNC2Stage() and invoked by setting #HyPar::spatial_type_par
86  to #_NC_2STAGE_: The parabolic term is discretized as
87 \f{equation}{
88  {\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) \approx \sum_{d1=0}^{D-1}\sum_{d2=0}^{D-1} \frac {1}{\Delta x_{d1} \Delta x_{d2}} \left[ \mathcal{D}_{d1}\left(\mathcal{D}_{d2}\left({\bf g}_d\right)\right) \right]
89 \f}
90  where \f$\mathcal{D}_d\f$ denotes the finite-difference first derivative operator along spatial dimension \f$d\f$, and is computed using #HyPar::FirstDerivativePar.
91 
92 + Non-conservative, "1.5-Stage" discretization, implemented in ParabolicFunctionNC1_5Stage() and invoked by setting #HyPar::spatial_type_par
93  to #_NC_1_5STAGE_: The parabolic term is discretized as
94 \f{equation}{
95  {\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) \approx \sum_{d1=0}^{D-1}\sum_{d2=0,d2 \ne d1}^{D-1} \frac {1}{\Delta x_{d1} \Delta x_{d2}} \left[ \mathcal{D}_{d1}\left(\mathcal{D}_{d2}\left({\bf g}_d\right)\right) \right]
96  + \sum_{d=0}^{D-1} \frac {1}{\Delta x_d^2} \left[ \mathcal{L}_d\left({\bf g}_d\right) \right]
97 \f}
98  where \f$\mathcal{D}_d\f$ denotes the finite-difference first derivative operator along spatial dimension \f$d\f$ (computed using #HyPar::FirstDerivativePar).
99  and \f$\mathcal{L}\f$ represents the finite-difference Laplacian operator (computed using #HyPar::SecondDerivativePar).
100 
101 The function pointers #HyPar::InterpolateInterfacesPar, #HyPar::SecondDerivativePar, and #HyPar::FirstDerivativePar are set in InitializeSolvers() based on the value of
102 #HyPar::spatial_scheme_par (read from \a "solver.inp" in ReadInputs()).
103 
104 Depending on which of the above forms are used, #HyPar::ParabolicFunction points to either of ParabolicFunctionCons1Stage(), ParabolicFunctionNC1Stage(),
105 ParabolicFunctionNC2Stage(), or ParabolicFunctionNC1_5Stage() (set in InitializeSolvers()).

107 Source term
108 -----------
109 
110 #HyPar::SourceFunction points to SourceFunction(). There is no discretization involved in general, and this function
111 just calls the physical model-specific source function to which #HyPar::SFunction points.
