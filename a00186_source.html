1 Numerical Method
2 ================
3 
4 HyPar solves the following partial differential equation (PDE) using a conservative finite-difference
5 algorithm on a Cartesian grid.
6 \f{equation}{
7  \frac {\partial {\bf u}} {\partial t} = {\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right) + {\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) + {\bf F}_{\rm sou}\left({\bf u}\right)
8 \f}
9 where \f${\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\f$ is the hyperbolic term, \f${\bf F}_{\rm par}\f$ is the parabolic term, and
10 \f${\bf F}_{\rm sou}\f$ is the source term. Each of these is discretized in space as described below (in
11 the section "Spatial Discretization"), to
12 obtain the following semi-discrete ordinary differential equation (ODE) in time:
13 \f{equation}{
14  \frac {d {\bf u}} {d t} = \hat{\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right) + \hat{\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) + \hat{\bf F}_{\rm sou}\left({\bf u}\right)
15 \f}
16 where \f$\hat{\left(\cdot\right)}\f$ represents the spatially discretized terms. The governing PDE can be
17 of any space dimension.

19 \section spatial_discretization Spatial Discretization
20 
21 Hyperbolic term
22 ---------------
23 
24 The hyperbolic term is of the following form:
25 \f{equation}{
26  {\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right) = -\sum_{d=0}^{D-1} \frac{\partial {\bf f}_d\left({\bf u}\right)}{\partial x_d}
27 \f}
28 and is discretized as:
29 \f{equation}{
30  {\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right) \approx - \sum_{d=0}^{D-1} \frac{1}{\Delta x_d} \left[ \hat{\bf f}_{d,j+1/2} - \hat{\bf f}_{d,j-1/2} \right]
31 \f}
32 where \f$d\f$ is the spatial dimension, \f$D\f$ is the total number of spatial dimensions, \f$j\f$ denotes the grid index along \f$d\f$. This
33 is implemented in HyperbolicFunction().
34 
35 The numerical approximation \f$\hat{\bf f}_{d,j+1/2}\f$ of the primitive of the flux \f${\bf f}_d\left({\bf u}\right)\f$ at the grid interfaces \f$j+1/2\f$ is expressed
36 as
37 \f{equation}{
38  \hat{\bf f}_{d,j+1/2} = \mathcal{U}\left(\hat{\bf f}^L_{d,j+1/2},\hat{\bf f}^R_{d,j+1/2},\hat{\bf u}^L_{d,j+1/2},\hat{\bf u}^R_{d,j+1/2}\right)
39 \f}
40 where \f$\mathcal{U}\f$ is an upwinding function. #HyPar::Upwind points to the physical model-specific upwinding function that implements
41 \f$\mathcal{U}\f$, and is set by the initialization function of a specific physical model (for example Euler1DInitialize()). The physical model
42 is specified by setting #HyPar::model (read from \a "solver.inp" in ReadInputs()).
43 
44 \f$\hat{\bf f}^{L,R}_{d,j+1/2}\f$ are the left- and right-biased numerically interpolated values of the primitive of the flux \f${\bf f}_d\left({\bf u}\right)\f$
45 at the grid interfaces and are computed using #HyPar::InterpolateInterfacesHyp. They are initialized in InitializeSolvers() based on the value of
46 #HyPar::spatial_scheme_hyp (read from \a "solver.inp" in ReadInputs()). See interpolation.h for all the spatial discretization schemes implemented.
47 
48 #HyPar::HyperbolicFunction points to HyperbolicFunction().
49 
50 Parabolic term
51 --------------
52 
53 The parabolic term can take two different forms as described below:-
54 
55 \b No \b cross-derivatives: In this form, the parabolic term is of the following form:
56 \f{equation}{
57  {\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) = \sum_{d=0}^{D-1} \frac {\partial^2 {\bf g}_d\left({\bf u}\right)} {\partial x_d^2}
58 \f}
59 where \f$d\f$ is the spatial dimension, and \f$D\f$ is the total number of spatial dimensions. If the parabolic function is
60 in this form, then the physical model must specify #HyPar::GFunction which must point to the function that computes
61 \f${\bf g}_d\left({\bf u}\right)\f$. In this case, the spatial discretization is carried out in one of two ways:-
62 
63 + Conservative discretization, implemented in ParabolicFunctionCons1Stage() and invoked by setting #HyPar::spatial_type_par
64  to #_CONS_1STAGE_: The parabolic term is discretized as
65 \f{equation}{
66  {\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) \approx \sum_{d=0}^{D-1} \frac {1}{\Delta x_d^2} \left[ \hat{\bf g}_{d,j+1/2} - \hat{\bf g}_{d,j-1/2} \right]
67 \f}
68  where \f$j\f$ denotes the grid index along \f$d\f$. \f$\hat{\bf g}_{d,j+1/2}\f$ is the numerical approximation to the second primitive
69  of \f${\bf g}_d\left({\bf u}\right)\f$ and is computed using #HyPar::InterpolateInterfacesPar.
70 
71 + Non-conservative, 1-Stage discretization, implemented in ParabolicFunctionNC1Stage() and invoked by setting #HyPar::spatial_type_par
72  to #_NC_1STAGE_: The parabolic term is discretized as
73 \f{equation}{
74  {\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) \approx \sum_{d=0}^{D-1} \frac {1}{\Delta x_d^2} \left[ \mathcal{L}_d\left({\bf g}_d\right) \right]
75 \f}
76  where \f$\mathcal{L}\f$ represents the finite-difference Laplacian operator, and is computed using #HyPar::SecondDerivativePar.
77 
78 \b With \b cross-derivatives: In this form, the parabolic term is of the following form:
79 \f{equation}{
80  {\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) = \sum_{d1=0}^{D-1}\sum_{d2=0}^{D-1} \frac {\partial^2 h_{d1,d2}\left({\bf u}\right)} {\partial x_{d1} \partial x_{d2}}
81 \f}
82 where \f$d1,d2\f$ are spatial dimensions, \f$D\f$ is the total number of spatial dimensions. If the parabolic function is
83 in this form, then the physical model must specify #HyPar::HFunction which must point to the function that computes
84 \f${\bf h}_{d1,d2}\left({\bf u}\right)\f$. In this case, the spatial discretization is carried out in one of two ways:-
85 
86 + Non-conservative 2-stage discretization, implemented in ParabolicFunctionNC2Stage() and invoked by setting #HyPar::spatial_type_par
87  to #_NC_2STAGE_: The parabolic term is discretized as
88 \f{equation}{
89  {\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) \approx \sum_{d1=0}^{D-1}\sum_{d2=0}^{D-1} \frac {1}{\Delta x_{d1} \Delta x_{d2}} \left[ \mathcal{D}_{d1}\left(\mathcal{D}_{d2}\left({\bf g}_d\right)\right) \right]
90 \f}
91  where \f$\mathcal{D}_d\f$ denotes the finite-difference first derivative operator along spatial dimension \f$d\f$, and is computed using #HyPar::FirstDerivativePar.
92 
93 + Non-conservative, "1.5-Stage" discretization, implemented in ParabolicFunctionNC1_5Stage() and invoked by setting #HyPar::spatial_type_par
94  to #_NC_1_5STAGE_: The parabolic term is discretized as
95 \f{equation}{
96  {\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) \approx \sum_{d1=0}^{D-1}\sum_{d2=0,d2 \ne d1}^{D-1} \frac {1}{\Delta x_{d1} \Delta x_{d2}} \left[ \mathcal{D}_{d1}\left(\mathcal{D}_{d2}\left({\bf g}_d\right)\right) \right]
97  + \sum_{d=0}^{D-1} \frac {1}{\Delta x_d^2} \left[ \mathcal{L}_d\left({\bf g}_d\right) \right]
98 \f}
99  where \f$\mathcal{D}_d\f$ denotes the finite-difference first derivative operator along spatial dimension \f$d\f$ (computed using #HyPar::FirstDerivativePar).
100  and \f$\mathcal{L}\f$ represents the finite-difference Laplacian operator (computed using #HyPar::SecondDerivativePar).
101 
102 The function pointers #HyPar::InterpolateInterfacesPar, #HyPar::SecondDerivativePar, and #HyPar::FirstDerivativePar are set in InitializeSolvers() based on the value of
103 #HyPar::spatial_scheme_par (read from \a "solver.inp" in ReadInputs()). See interpolation.h, firstderivative.h, and secondderivative.h for the spatial
104 disretization methods implemented.
105 
106 Depending on which of the above forms are used, #HyPar::ParabolicFunction points to either of ParabolicFunctionCons1Stage(), ParabolicFunctionNC1Stage(),
107 ParabolicFunctionNC2Stage(), or ParabolicFunctionNC1_5Stage() (set in InitializeSolvers()).

109 Source term
110 -----------
111 
112 #HyPar::SourceFunction points to SourceFunction(). There is no discretization involved in general, and this function
113 just calls the physical model-specific source function to which #HyPar::SFunction points.

116 \section time_integration Time Integration
117 
118 Native Time Integrators
119 -----------------------
120 
121 The semi-discrete ODE is integrated in time using explicit multi-stage time integration methods. The ODE can be written as:
122 \f{equation}{
123  \frac {d {\bf u}} {d t} = {\bf F}\left({\bf u}\right)
124 \f}
125 where
126 \f{equation}{
127  {\bf F}\left({\bf u}\right) = \hat{\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right) + \hat{\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right) + \hat{\bf F}_{\rm sou}\left({\bf u}\right)
128 \f}
129 The following explicit time integration methods are implemented in HyPar (see timeintegration.h):
130 + Forward Euler - TimeForwardEuler(), #_FORWARD_EULER_
131 + Explicit Runge-Kutta - TimeRK(), #_RK_
132 + Explicit General Linear Methods with Global Error Estimation - TimeGLMGEE(), #_GLM_GEE_

137 PETSc Time Integrators
138 ----------------------
139 
140 If compiled with PETSc (https://www.mcs.anl.gov/petsc/), HyPar can use all the time integration methods and features implemented in the \b TS module of
141 PETSc. See the following for relevant documentation of PETSc time integrators:
142 + http://www.mcs.anl.gov/petsc/petsc-current/docs/manualpages/TS/index.html
143 + http://www.mcs.anl.gov/petsc/petsc-current/src/ts/examples/tutorials/index.html
144 + http://www.mcs.anl.gov/petsc/petsc-current/docs/manual.pdf (Chapter 6: Scalable ODE and DAE Solvers)
145 
146 In addition to explicit time integration, the semi-discrete ODE can be solved using
147 + \b Implicit \b methods (Eg. backward Euler (TSBEULER - http://www.mcs.anl.gov/petsc/petsc-current/docs/manualpages/TS/TSBEULER.html),
148 Crank-Nicholson (TSCN - http://www.mcs.anl.gov/petsc/petsc-current/docs/manualpages/TS/TSCN.html#TSCN), \f$\theta\f$-method (TSTHETA - http://www.mcs.anl.gov/petsc/petsc-current/docs/manualpages/TS/TSTHETA.html#TSTHETA),
149 etc.)
150 + <B>Semi-implicit (IMEX) methods</B> (TSARKIMEX - http://www.mcs.anl.gov/petsc/petsc-current/docs/manualpages/TS/TSARKIMEX.html)
151 
152 Implementation: see petscinterface.h and SolvePETSc().
153 
154 \b Implicit and \b IMEX time integration:
155 + The Jacobian-free approach is used to compute the Jacobian of the implicit term (i.e., the action of the Jacobian on a vector
156  is approximated using a directional derivative). Use the flag <B>-jfnk_epsilon \<value\></B> to specify the parameter \f$\epsilon\f$
157  for the directional derivative computation (default: \f$10^{-6}\f$). See PetscJacobianFunctionIMEX_JFNK(), PetscJacobianFunction_JFNK().
158 + A preconditioner can only be used for physical models that define the Jacobian (#HyPar::JFunction)
159  (for example, LinearADRJacobian(), Euler1DJacobian(), NavierStokes2DJacobian(), NavierStokes3DJacobian(), etc).
160  The flag <B>-with_pc</B> should be specified to use a preconditioner.
161 
162 <B>IMEX Time Integration</B>: For implicit-explicit (IMEX) time integration, the semi-discrete ODE can be written as follows:
163 \f{equation}{
164  \frac {d {\bf u}} {d t} = {\bf F}\left({\bf u}\right) + {\bf G}\left({\bf u}\right)
165 \f}
166 where \f${\bf F}\left({\bf u}\right)\f$ is integrated explicitly in time and \f${\bf G}\left({\bf u}\right)\f$
167 is integrated implicitly in time. The following flags (specified in the command line or in the <B>.petscrc</B>
168 file) can be used to specify which of the hyperbolic, parabolic, or source terms are treated explicitly, and
169 which are treated implicitly.
170 
171 Term | Explicit | Implicit
172 ------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------|---------------------
173 Hyperbolic \f$\hat{\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right)\f$ | -hyperbolic_explicit | -hyperbolic_implicit
174 Parabolic \f$\hat{\bf F}_{\rm par}\left({\bf u}\right)\f$ | -parabolic_explicit | -parabolic_implicit
175 Source \f$\hat{\bf F}_{\rm sou}\left({\bf u}\right)\f$ | -source_explicit | -source_implicit
176 
177 + If contradictory flags are specified, i.e.,
178 
179  -parabolic_explicit -parabolic_implicit
180 
181  the flag specifying implicit treatment takes precedence.
182 
183 + In addition, if a partitioning of the hyperbolic flux is defined and is being used (#HyPar::SplitHyperbolicFlux),
184  i.e,
185  \f{equation}{
186  \hat{\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right) = \left[\hat{\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right) - \delta\hat{\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right)\right] + \delta\hat{\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right)
187  \f}
188  the following flags can be used to specify which of these two terms are treated explicitly and which are treated implicitly.
189 
190  Term | Explicit | Implicit
191  ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------|---------------------
192  \f$\left[\hat{\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right) - \delta\hat{\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right)\right]\f$ | -hyperbolic_f_explicit | -hyperbolic_f_implicit
193  \f$\delta\hat{\bf F}_{\rm hyp}\left({\bf u}\right)\f$ | -hyperbolic_df_explicit | -hyperbolic_df_implicit
